About

Hi! My name is Daniel, and I'm an Engineer. I actually started out college as an Exercise Science major of all things. At the time, I was working as a CrossFit coach, which was a cool first job. But since I didn't really know what I wanted to do with my career, I thought maybe fitness be a good place to start. After a few classes, however, I realized that really wasn't a good fit for me. So I moved back up to Seattle and try out Mechanical Engineering next. Being a transfer student, my credits and schedule weren't perfectly aligned, which meant I needed to take 1 extra class my final quarter of my senior year to keep my financial aid. This led me to take Java Programming on a whim, and I ended up really liking it. So, thus far in my career I have continued to pursue projects in both software and mechanical engineering whenever I've gotten the opportunity.

Fitness

Ahhhhh, I feel like such a has-been taking about my fitness career, but here goes... I'll start by saying: I can't do what I used to! I worked for 9 years as a coach at CrossFit Bellevue, CrossFit Peak Performance, and Lake Hills CrossFit. During that time, I was a 2 time NorthWest Regional competitor, reaching 14th overall in Washington, Oregon, Idaho, Montana and Alaska. I could clean and jerk over 300 pounds, and snatch 245. But don't get me wrong, I'm not very big... I'm only 5'5" and weigh 170 pounds. What I liked most about fitness was not the work, or the adrenaline, or the way I looked. It was the biomechanics. Being able to take advantage of physics to beat bigger, stronger guys by being more efficient gave me some silly sense of satisfaction. It's probably why I ended up liking Engineering a lot more than Exercise Science.
